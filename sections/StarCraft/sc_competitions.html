STARCRAFT II WCS

The StarCraft II World Championship Series (WCS) was a StarCraft II professional tournament series organized by Blizzard Entertainment.
It ran from 2012 to 2019 and for all years of its operation except one, it was the highest tier of StarCraft II Esports.
For its first year, the Grand Finals were held in Shanghai, China. For all other years, Grand finals were held in Anaheim, California.
All events were also streamed on Twitch and Youtube, allowing fans from around the world to spectate.

The prize pool for the WCS Global Finals was $700,000 and was split amongst the highest 32 ranking players at end of the competition.
The 1st, 2nd and 3rd place contestants received $210,000, $96,000 and $51,000 respectively.

Throughout the operation of WCS, StarCraft II remained one of the most viewed, sponsored, and active Esport in the world.

ESL PRO TOUR

The Electronic Sports League (ESL) is an Esports league that was founded in 1997.
It has over 2.6 million registered members and is one of the biggest gaming leagues. The ESL organises several StarCraft II events.

In January 2020 ESL announced the ESL Pro Tour (EPT), which was created to replace the Blizzard's WCS System.
The ESL Pro Tour is a combination of the legacies of ESL, DreamHack and WCS tournaments.

For 2020/21, the ESL system will be split into two: ESL Pro Tour Circuit and ESL Pro Tour Korea.
Both systems will operate individually, with each systems having its own standings and points for the various events that take place.
The ESL Pro Tour Korea will consist of players from South Korea, while ESL Pro Tour Circuit will invite players from the rest of the world.

There is a prize pool of over $1.9 million across the tours and the competition crowns the official StarCraft II World Champions.
