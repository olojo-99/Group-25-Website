ALPHASTAR

AlphaStar is a computer program created by DeepMind, a subsidary of Google, that was designed to play StarCraft II.
The project was unveiled to the public by name in January 2019.
AlphaStar is considered to be a major milestone in Artificial Intelligence as it achieved Grandmaster status in August 2019.
This is considered to be incredibly difficult due to the complexity of the game.

To create AlphaStar, Deepmind trained three separate neural networks - one for each race of aliens it played as.
AlphaStar then accessed the vast database of past games provided by Starcraft's developer Blizzard.
This allowed AlphaStar's agents to be trained to imitate the moves of the game's strongest players.
Duplicates of these copies then competed against each other to hone their abilities, this is known as Reinforcement Learning.
Exploiter agents were also created to expose weaknesses in the gameplay of the main agents so that it could be improved upon.

This process spanned over 44 days. However, due to the high speed that the games were carried out at, it represented about 200 years of human gameplay.
The resulting three neural networks then anonymously competed against human players on Blizzard's Battle.net platform.
After a series of intense games, all three neural networks attained Grandmaster staus for each of the three alien races.
This was considered to be an immense achievement, as Grandmaster status is only given to the top players around the world.

DeepMind aim to use the techniques deployed in AlphaStar to advance their research in real-world domains.
