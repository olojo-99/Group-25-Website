TEAM LIQUID

Team Liquid is a pro gaming team that was founded in November 2000 by Victor Goossens.
A clan for StarCraft II was created in 2010 and Team Liquid subsequently signed their first professional players.
Soon after, Team Liquid became one of the most successful teams, winning four DreamHacks, two MLGs, two Assemblies, and one NASL.

After the release of StarCraft II, the team signed a partnership agreement with Old Generations, a South Korean organisation.
This allowed Team Liquid to become the first foreign team to be involved in the Korean StarCraft scene on a regular basis.

The team's StarCraft II roster currently consists of six players and has a diverse national pool, with players from Germany, Poland, Netherlands, South Korea and France.

JIN AIR GREEN WINGS

Jin Air Green Wings is the StarCraft II team of South Korean airline Jin Air, which flies domestically within South Korea and also to China, Japan, Guam and Southeast Asia.
The organisation was formed in 2011 and was formerly known as the 8th Team.
After being bought by Jin Air in 2013, the team was renamed Jin Air Green Wings.

The team has earned over $2.5million in over 379 StarCraft tournaments, making it the highest earning StarCraft II team of all time.

SK TELECOM T1

SK Telecom T1 is a Korean esports team owned and named after the South Korean telecommunications company SK Telecom.
The team was formed in 2002 under the name Orion and was renamed in 2003 after being sponsored by SK Telecom.

On May 2012, the team was introduced to StarCraft II through the hybrid 2011-2012 Proleague Season 2, a professional Esports league.
Since then, team has earned over $1.1millon in over 94 StarCraft tournaments.
