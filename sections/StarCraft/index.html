STARCRAFT II: WINGS OF LIBERTY

StarCraft II: Wings of Liberty is a science fiction real-time strategy game that was developed by Blizzard Entertainment. It was released worldwide in July 2010 for Microsoft Windows and Mac OS X.
The game is split into three installments: Wings of Liberty (the Terran campaign), Heart of the Swarm (the Zerg campaign) and Legacy of the Void (the Protoss campaign).
In 2016, Blizzard released StarCraft II: Nova Covert Ops, a series of 9 missions released over a trio of installments.

StarCraft II is a sequal to 1998 release StarCraft and like its predecessor consists of three races: Terran, Zerg and Protoss.
The Terrans are Humans that have been exiled from Earth, The Zerg are a race of insectoid aliens and the Protoss are a technologically advanced humanoid species.
The game is also set four years after the events of Blizzard's 1998 Brood War and includes new and returning characters and locations from the original game.

GAMEPLAY

The StarCraft II experience places emphasis on the three playable races locked in intra-galactic warfare.
This narrative can be explored through a story campaign, competitive online multiplayer and collaborative co-op missions.

In any given match, the objective is to destroy your opponent's structures and units using your own fighting forces.
At the start of each match, each player is given worker units who are capable of gathering basic mineral resources.
These can be used to start harvesting other materials and build increasingly powerful units and structures.
There is also a supply cap, which forces players to build supply structures in order to continue building new units.

Each race has its own tech tree which can be explored as players make progress in a match.
A tech tree allows you to build new structures and units.

StarCraft II features approximately the same number of units as the original StarCraft.
Many units from the original game were brought to the sequal, some featuring new upgrades and abilities.

RECEPTION

StarCraft II has been met with critical acclaim since its release.
It was rated 93/100 by Metacritic and PC Gamer, and also received an aggregrate score of 92.39% at GameRankings.
In particular, the game was praised for retaining the Real Time Strategy gameplay from the original game, while introducing new features and improved storytelling.

In its first month on sale, StarCraft II sold a total of three million copies worldwide.

StarCraft II: Wings of Liberty placed 26th on Time's "The 50 Best Video Games of All Time" list in August 2016.
