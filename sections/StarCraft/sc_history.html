BLIZZARD ENTERTAINMENT

Blizzard Entertainment is an American company based in Irvine, California.
They are known for developing video games such as World of Warcraft, Overwatch, Warcraft, the Diablo series and the StarCraft series.
The company is a subsidary of Activision Blizzard and was founded on February 8th, 1991.

Blizzard Entertainment was founded by Michael Morhaime, Allen Adham, and Frank Pearce, who all earned their bachelor's degrees from UCLA in 1990.
The company was originally named Silicon & Synapse before undergoing a series of name changes.
The founders finally renamed the company "Blizzard Entertainment" in May 1994

Since 1991, Blizzard Entertainment has developed 19 games, with 11 of those games being within the the Warcraft, Diablo, and StarCraft series.
Since the releases of Warcraft: Orcs & Humans (1994), Diablo (1997), and StarCraft (1998), Blizzard has focused almost exclusively on those three series.

STARCRAFT

Planning for the development of the original StarCraft game began in 1995, shortly after the release of Warcraft II.

StarCraft was released internationally on March 31st, 1998 and subsequently became the best-selling PC game for that year, with over 1.5 million sales worldwide.
And by May 2007, StarCraft had sold over 9.5 million copies around the world, with 4.5 million copies being sold in South Korea.
On the 1st November 2010, StarCraft received the Guinness World Record for "best selling strategy game for PC" and "longest-serving Esports game".

Soon after its release, the game received cult-like status in the computer gaming world.
StarCraft still remains popular today due to its complexity and depth of the strategic possibilities.
In South Korea, the series popularity has been especially high, with nationally recognized tournaments and intense training camps across the country.
Live tournaments are often televised, with top players receiving mild celebrity status.

DEVELOPMENT OF STARCRAFT II

On May 19th 2007, at the Blizzard Worldwide Invitational in Seoul, South Korea, Blizzard Entertainment announced the development of StarCraft II.

The team devoted to StarCraft II consisted of 40 developers, including 12 developers who had worked on the original game.
As of 2008, the team consisted of four or five managers, 12-14 programmers, 8-10 artists and around 10 designers, including lead designers Kevin Dong and Ryan Schutter.
By June of 2009, the team had expanded to 50 members and by August of the same year it had grew to a further 60 members.

Compared to its precursor, StarCraft II was designed with a primary focus on the multiplayer aspect, with one of the main goals being to recreate the multiplayer of the original game in 3D.
Developers placed an emphasis on designing the game specifically around competitive, skill-based Esports.
A new competitive ladder system for ranked games and new matchmaking mechanics used to match players of equal skill level were also integrated into the game.

During development, there were five levels of AI difficulty created for custom games: Beginner, Easy, Moderate, Hard, and Insane.
This gradually expanded to ten levels: Very Easy, Easy, Medium, Hard, Harder, Very Hard, Elite, and three different Cheater options.
