INTRODUCTION

Tom Clancy’s Rainbow Six: Siege is a 5 v 5 tactical shooter where attackers enter a building and try to plant a defuser to de-activate the bombs while the defenders try to prevent them from carrying out their objective until the time runs out.
This is the bare structure of the game, very similar to Counter Strike and Valorant and the like except it has a twist that exponentially changes the formula; the environmental destructibility.

Every map and building in the game have a very high level of destructibility where almost all the floors, ceilings and walls are destructible where you can shoot holes through them which creates a bunch of angles and lines of sights which you can take advantage of.
If you shoot a wall with a pistol, it will create tiny bullet holes, while if you shoot a wall with a shotgun, it will create a much larger hole, etc.

On top of the destructibility where there are limitless possibilities, there is also a hero system implemented where the people that you play, known as “Operators”, have unique gadgets and loadouts that will turn the tide of the game.
Each operator has a primary and secondary weapon, which are mostly unique to that one operator, as well as a secondary gadget that you can use, i.e. barbed wire which slows attackers down, smoke grenades which cuts lines of sites so you can access areas without being shot at, or even utility as simple as frag grenades for the attackers to get a defender out of a certain spot without putting yourself in danger.

With all these mechanics put together, it makes an experience or a certain mindset like no other game and therefore, puts Siege into a genre in itself as there is no other game that delivers an experience like this.
