TOURNAMENTS

Overview

There are many different kinds of CS:GO tournaments, from the smaller online tournaments to the more significant offline tournsments with larger prize pools. These large tournaments are known as S-Tier tournaments and takeplace all throughout the year, with the majority of them following a seasonal format, for example the Intel Extreme Masters.
Most tournaments are run by separate third parties, for example the ESL Pro League tournament first have a qualifying stage in which teams from Europe play one another and teams from North and South A merica play one another and the top 4 teams in eachthen advance to the finals in which they all play for a massive prize pool.
Prize pools from these s-tier tournaments can range from $100,000 to $1,000,000 depending on the tournament

An excited fan cheering his team on.

Majors

The most important tournament by far has to be the Major tournaments which take place twice a year (formerly three).
These are sponsored by Valve and have a price pool of $1,000,000. Majors are made up of 24 teams. 4 minors are played throughout the year in Asia, Europe, CIS and the Americas, The top 2 teams from each of these qualify to be in the Majors while the other 8 are the top 8 teams from the last Major.
These events are watched by millions of people and are watched all across the world. Astralis is the winner of the most number of Majors, having won 4, and also being the last team to win the Major.
