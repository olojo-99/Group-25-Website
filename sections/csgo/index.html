COUNTER STRIKE:GLOBAL OFFENSIVE

A Brief Introduction

Counter Strike: Global Offensive (CS:GO) is a multiplayer FPS in which there are two teams. One team acts as the terrorists and tries to plant a bomb on one of either two sites. A or B. They then have to defend the bomb from the opposing team, the Counter-Terrorists.
The Counter-Terroristsâ€™ goal is to stop the terrorist from detonating the bomb by either killing all the terrorists before they plant the bomb or defuse it. This is the main concept of CS:GO and seems is fairly easy to understand but incredibly difficult to master.
This is what makes the competitive scene in CS:GO one of the most popular eSports in all of gaming, with prize pools of up to $1,000,000.

Unique Gameplay Aspects

Unlike most other multiplayer FPS games guns are bought using money. This money is earned after every round of the game (unless a terrorist survives after the round ends without having planted the bomb) and is also earned by killing players on the enemy, assisting in killing players on the enemy team, defusing the bomb, planting the bomb, picking up the hostage and rescuing the hostage.
The amount of money earned for a kill also depends on the weapon used. It is a very in-depth mechanic with a lot of strategy involved, especially in high-level play.
Before every round begins there is a buy period in which players can buy weapons, armor and other utilities. This function is represented by a buy wheel which players can use to find the weapons, armor and utilities.
