Dota 2

INTRODUCTION

Over the last 10 years, MOBA (Multiplayer Online Battle Arena) games have taken the PC market by storm. Dota is one of these, but it is not just any MOBA. Dota is arguably the most important MOBA, the most impactful. With a history of over 16 years, and tournament prize pools that can change lives, it truly is something special.

Dota 2 map

THE GAME

Like most other games in its genre, every game is played on a square shaped map, with one team’s base in the top right corner, and the others team’s in the bottom left. Each team has 5 players, and they each chose a hero from a pool of 119. And then, the battle begins. First team to destroy the other team’s “Ancient” wins.

BACKGROUND

Dota is developed by Valve Software, the creators of some of the best games of all time on PC such as the Half Life series, and the Portal games. They released the game in Beta form in 2011, and players have been enjoying the game for free ever since. Every hero and feature in the game are completely free, a brand-new player could pick it up and have access to everything in a few games (when they pass the tutorial). And yet despite this free-to-play model, the game is very lucrative for Valve. How do they achieve this? In-game Cosmetics. Players can spend money to dress up their heroes, make them have fancy effects and animations. This years Battlepass Compendium sales alone added up to $160 million dollars. Usually, ¼ of this money would be used to fund the prize pool of Valve’s annual tournament, The International, but this year it was cancelled due to the pandemic.
